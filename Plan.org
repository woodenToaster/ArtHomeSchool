* Art Goals Mind Map & 4-Week Term Schedule

Two primary tracks for achieving my goals:

1. **Learn** — Build foundational skills through studies, copies, and exercises.
2. **Create** — Apply skills in original artwork and finished pieces.

A balanced practice plan should avoid getting trapped in endless studies while
still building skill. A useful balance is:

- **50% Study / Learning**
- **25% Skill Exercises**
- **25% Original Art**

This keeps growth balanced while still producing meaningful artwork.

** Long-Term Learning Order

Fundamentals depend on each other. A useful progression is:

```text
Gesture
   ↓
Perspective
   ↓
Structure
   ↓
Anatomy
   ↓
Light & Value
   ↓
Color
   ↓
Composition
   ↓
Finished Illustration
```

The highest-priority foundations are:

1. Gesture
2. Perspective
3. Anatomy
4. Light / Value

These four areas support almost everything else in your goals.

---

** 4-Week Term Structure

Each term should include a mix of warmups, study, focused exercises, and original art.

*** Daily Session Structure

**** Warmup — 15%

- Quick sketches
- Gestures
- Line exercises

**** Study — 35%

- Master studies
- Photo copies
- Watts assignments
- Anatomy studies

**** Exercise — 25%

- Perspective drills
- Invented gestures
- Shadow mapping
- Structural drawing

**** Create — 25%

- Original artwork
- Illustration process work
- Personal pieces

* Success Metrics

Each 4-week term should produce:

- 20–40 gestures
- 8–12 studies
- 4–8 perspective exercises
- 2–4 anatomy studies
- 1 master study
- 1 finished artwork

After 4 terms, or 16 weeks, you should have:

- Stronger drawing fundamentals
- Figure and anatomy experience
- Environment and architecture practice
- Experience with the full illustration workflow
- 4 polished portfolio-quality pieces

Most importantly, this plan keeps study and creation happening together, which
is the fastest path toward becoming the kind of artist described in your goals.
